Introduction: The Core Philosophy

Welcome to the AI Storytelling Assistant! This application is built on one core idea: a smarter AI companion is one that you teach.

Instead of relying on a generic AI, this tool uses a powerful Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) system. This means the AI's "brain" is your own custom knowledge base. The more detail you provide about your fictional universe, the more consistent, creative, and context-aware its suggestions will be. The foundation of this knowledge base is the World.

Step 1: The Foundation - Your World

Everything starts with a World. A World is the container for your universe's rules, history, and inhabitants. It is the single source of truth for the AI.

Creating Your First World

You have three powerful ways to create a world from the My Worlds page:

  • Create New World: Start with a blank slate. Give your universe a name and a description, and begin building from scratch.
  • Import from Book Title: The fastest way to get started. Enter a title like The Hobbit or Dune, and the AI will generate a foundational world complete with key characters, locations, and lore to give you a massive head start.
  • Create from Document: Upload a document (e.g., a story bible, an early draft). The AI will read it and attempt to extract all the world elements automatically.
⏱️ Job Tracking
Importing a world is a background job that can take a few minutes. The application will show you a real-time status so you can track its progress without waiting.

Populating Your World: The 3 Core Elements

Once a World is created, its true power comes from the elements you define within it. Every time you create or update one of these, the AI learns more about your universe.

  • Characters: The "who." Define their personalities, backstories, and appearances. The more detail here, the better the AI can write their dialogue and actions true to their nature.
  • Locations: The "where." Describe the atmosphere, appearance, and significance of key places. This helps the AI generate immersive, sensory descriptions.
  • Lore Items: The "what" and "why." This is where you define the rules of your universe—magic systems, historical events, unique artifacts, or political factions. This is how you teach the AI what is possible (and impossible) in your world.

Step 2: The Narrative - Your Story

A Story is your main creative project (a novel, screenplay, etc.). From the My Stories page, click "Create New Story."

Linking Your Story to a World

This is the most important step in creating a story. You must associate your story with one of your existing Worlds. This link is what enables the AI to access all the characters, locations, and lore you've built.

After creating the story, you can further refine the context on the Story Detail page by linking the specific elements from your world that will be featured in that narrative. This helps the AI focus on the most relevant information.

Step 3: Writing with Your AI Co-Author

The Act Editor is your primary workspace. Here, you can write your narrative and collaborate with the AI.

Content Generation

In the "AI Assistant" tab, you have full control over the AI's creative process:

  • Generation Mode: Choose how the AI should interact with your text. You can have it Continue/Append to your writing, Rewrite a highlighted section, or Elaborate/Expand on a selected idea.
  • Your Instruction: Be specific! Instead of "write more," try "Describe the tavern brawl from the perspective of the frightened bartender."
  • Incorporate: The AI's response appears in a preview box. If you like it, click "Incorporate" to add it directly to your manuscript.

AI Review & Feedback

For a higher-level analysis, use the "Review with AI" feature. The AI will read your entire act and provide structured feedback on:

  • Suggestions: Actionable advice on pacing, character motivation, plot coherence, and more. You can even click an "Apply" button for some suggestions to automatically implement the change.
  • Metrics: A 1-5 rating across several categories like engagement, plot, and descriptive language, helping you identify areas for improvement.

Advanced Features

The RAG System: The AI's Memory

The AI's ability to remember your world comes from the RAG system. It's automatically fed by the Characters, Locations, and Lore Items you create. You can further enhance its knowledge by uploading your own documents (e.g., research notes, timelines, inspirational texts) via the Manage Documents page and associating them with a world.

The Prompt Library

The Prompt Library lets you save and reuse your most effective instructions for the AI. This is perfect for maintaining a consistent style or tone.

  • Instructional Prompts: Reusable instructions for the "Your Instruction" box.
  • System Prompts: Special prompts that define the AI's core persona (e.g., "You are a witty, fast-paced fantasy author"). You can assign a System Prompt to an Act to guide the AI's style for all generations within that act.
  • Image Style Prompts: Define consistent artistic styles (e.g., "cinematic, photorealistic") that can be applied when generating images for your world elements.
💡 Pro Tip
The key to success with this AI storytelling tool is patience in world-building. The time you invest in creating detailed Characters, Locations, and Lore Items will pay dividends in the quality and consistency of the AI's creative assistance. Start small, but think big!
