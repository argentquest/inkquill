Lore Overview

Lore items are the building blocks of your fictional world's history, culture, and unique elements. They can represent anything from magical artifacts and ancient prophecies to cultural customs and historical events. Each lore item becomes part of your world's searchable knowledge base, accessible to both you and your AI assistant.

Adding Details

Enrich your lore items with comprehensive details that bring your world to life:

Core Information

Name: The primary identifier for your lore item
Category: Classification for organization
Description: Detailed explanation of the item's nature and significance
Importance: Scale from minor detail to world-defining element

Extended Details

Origin: How, when, and where the lore item came to be
History: Evolution and changes over time
Current Status: Present-day relevance and condition
Significance: Impact on your world and story

Contextual Information

Timeline: When the lore item existed or was relevant
Geographic Scope: Where it has influence or presence
Cultural Impact: How it affects different societies
Secrets: Hidden aspects or mysteries

Relationships

Connect your lore items to create a rich, interconnected world:

Character Connections

Creators: Who made or established the lore item
Guardians: Who protects or maintains it
Users: Who interacts with or is affected by it
Seekers: Who desires or pursues it

Location Links

Origin Points: Where the lore item was created
Current Location: Where it exists now
Areas of Influence: Geographic regions it affects
Sacred Sites: Places of special significance

Lore Connections

Prerequisites: Other lore items that led to this one
Consequences: What resulted from this lore item
Related Items: Similar or connected lore elements
Conflicts: Opposing or contradictory lore

Media & Assets

Documents

In-World Texts: Books, scrolls, or documents about the lore

Audio Elements

Pronunciation Guides: How to say unique names or terms
Ambient Sounds: Audio that captures the lore's atmosphere
Music: Themes or melodies associated with the lore
Voice Notes: Your recorded thoughts and ideas

💡 Pro Tip

Great lore serves multiple purposes: it enriches your world, provides story hooks, and creates opportunities for character development. When creating lore, always ask "How can this impact my story or characters?" to ensure it serves your narrative goals.
