Beginner Understanding Story Structure

Stories in the AI Storytelling Assistant follow a hierarchical structure: Stories contain Acts, Acts contain Scenes. This organization helps both writers and AI maintain narrative coherence.

📖 Story Level

Overall narrative container with title, summary, genre, and tone settings.

🎭 Act Level

Major story divisions (Setup, Confrontation, Resolution) with their own themes and goals.

🎬 Scene Level

Individual dramatic units where specific events happen in specific locations with specific characters.

Creating Your Story Structure

10 minutes
  1. 1
    Fill in story metadata (title, description, genre, tone)
  2. 2
    Plan your act structure (typically 3-5 acts)
  3. 3
    Associate relevant world elements (characters, locations, lore)
  4. 4
    Break acts down into individual scenes
  5. 5
    Begin writing with AI assistance
💡
Writing Tip: Start with a simple three-act structure: Setup (25%), Confrontation (50%), Resolution (25%). You can always add complexity later.

Act Management

Acts represent major story divisions and help organize your narrative flow:

Act Type Purpose Typical Content
Act I - Setup Establish world and characters Character introduction, world-building, inciting incident
Act II - Confrontation Build tension and conflict Obstacles, character development, rising action
Act III - Resolution Resolve conflicts and conclude Climax, falling action, resolution
Adding and Managing Acts ▼
  • Add Act: Click "Add New Act" button or use Ctrl+Shift+A
  • Reorder Acts: Drag and drop to change sequence
  • Edit Act: Click act title or use the edit button
  • Delete Act: Use context menu (right-click) for removal options

Writing Best Practices

Start with Outlines

Plan act and scene structures before detailed writing. AI works better with clear direction.

Use World Elements

Associate relevant characters, locations, and lore with each scene for richer AI assistance.

Maintain Consistency

Review AI suggestions for character voice and world consistency before accepting.

Iterate and Refine

Use AI for first drafts, then edit and refine to match your vision.

Advanced AI-Powered Writing Assistance

As you write, AI provides:

Story Analysis Tools ▼

AI can analyze your story for:

  • Pacing Analysis: Evaluate narrative rhythm and tension
  • Theme Tracking: Ensure thematic elements are developed

RAG Context System

The Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) system ensures AI has access to your world knowledge:

  • Character Knowledge: AI remembers character traits, relationships, and history
  • World Consistency: References established lore, locations, and rules
  • Story Continuity: Maintains plot threads and character development
  • Contextual Relevance: Suggestions fit the current scene and act
🔍
How RAG Works: When you request AI assistance, the system searches your world elements and previous story content to provide contextually relevant suggestions.

Genre and Tone Guidelines ▼

Popular Genres:

  • Fantasy: Magic, mythical creatures, alternate worlds
  • Science Fiction: Technology, space, future societies
  • Mystery: Puzzles, investigation, revelation
  • Romance: Relationships, emotional connection, love
  • Thriller: Suspense, danger, fast pacing
  • Historical: Past settings, period accuracy, cultural context

Tone Options:

  • Dark: Serious, somber, pessimistic themes
  • Light: Upbeat, optimistic, humorous elements
  • Epic: Grand scale, heroic, larger-than-life
  • Intimate: Personal, character-focused, emotional
  • Satirical: Critical, ironic, social commentary
Story Statistics and Progress ▼

Track your story development with:

  • Word Count: Total words across all acts and scenes
  • Completion Percentage: How much of planned content is written

📊
Progress Tracking: Set target word counts for acts and scenes to monitor writing progress and maintain pacing goals.
